# Surveys FAQ

## Who can create a survey?

Users with **maintainer**, **board-member**, or **executive-director** personas can create surveys. Contributors can respond to surveys they receive.

## What is NPS and how does it work?

Net Promoter Score (NPS) measures how likely respondents are to recommend your project on a scale of 0 to 10. Respondents scoring 9–10 are "promoters," 7–8 are "passives," and 0–6 are "detractors." The NPS is calculated as the percentage of promoters minus the percentage of detractors.

## How do respondents access a survey?

Survey links are shared by the survey creator with respondents directly.

## Can I see individual survey responses?

Select a survey to view its responses and results. Use the survey settings to edit or close it.

## Can I edit a survey after it has been published?

Contact your project administrator to make changes to a published survey.

## How do I see which surveys are still open?

Use the **Open** and **Closed** tabs on the My Surveys page to filter surveys by status.

## Why don't I see any surveys?

Make sure you have the correct project selected in the lens switcher. Surveys are scoped to a project context.

## What is the difference between Surveys and Votes?

**Surveys** collect general feedback and opinions from a broad audience. **Votes** are formal decision-making polls used in governance contexts, such as committee elections or policy decisions.
